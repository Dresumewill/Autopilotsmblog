The economics of being a solopreneur have fundamentally changed.

Two years ago, scaling past $10K/month as a solo operator meant either burning out or hiring. Today, a smart AI stack can extend your personal leverage so far that one person can genuinely run what used to require a team of five.

I've spent months talking to solopreneurs doing $10K–$100K/month alone. Here's what the best of them have in common: a lean, intentional AI stack that covers every function of their business.

## The 5 Business Functions Every Solopreneur Needs to Cover

No matter what you sell, your business has five core functions:

1. **Marketing** — Getting found and building an audience
2. **Sales** — Converting interest into revenue
3. **Delivery** — Doing or producing the thing you sell
4. **Operations** — Admin, finance, legal, planning
5. **Support** — Serving and retaining customers

Most solopreneurs are great at one or two of these and mediocre (or terrible) at the rest. AI doesn't make you excellent at everything — but it can make you *good enough* at everything, which is all you need.

## The Stack

### 1. Marketing: Claude + Surfer SEO + Buffer

**Monthly cost**: ~$124
**Time saved**: 10–15 hours/week

Claude handles the creative heavy lifting — blog posts, email newsletters, social captions, ad copy, landing pages. Surfer ensures the content actually ranks. Buffer schedules and distributes it.

**Typical weekly workflow:**
- Monday: Use Claude to draft 1 blog post (based on Surfer keyword data)
- Tuesday: Use Claude to repurpose blog → newsletter + 5 social posts
- Wednesday: Load everything into Buffer for the week
- Thursday–Friday: Engage with comments, respond to DMs

**Key Claude prompt for solopreneurs:**
> "I'm a solopreneur who sells [product] to [audience]. Write me a [content type] about [topic] that sounds like it was written by someone who's been in the trenches, not a generic AI. Avoid corporate language. Be direct and specific."

---

### 2. Sales: Apollo.io + Clay + Claude

**Monthly cost**: ~$100–150
**Time saved**: 5–8 hours/week

If you do any B2B outreach, this combination is transformative.

**Apollo.io** finds your ideal prospects based on industry, company size, title, and more. **Clay** enriches contact data and automates personalized research. **Claude** writes outreach copy that sounds genuinely human.

**The workflow:**
1. Define your ideal customer profile in Apollo
2. Pull a list of 50–100 contacts per week
3. Use Clay to enrich with LinkedIn data, recent news, company info
4. Use Claude to generate a personalized first line for each prospect based on their data
5. Send sequences through Apollo

**Result**: Outreach that feels researched and personal — at scale.

For solopreneurs selling to consumers or through inbound, skip this and invest more in content marketing.

---

### 3. Delivery: Notion AI + Descript + Loom

**Monthly cost**: ~$40
**Time saved**: 4–6 hours/week

Delivery looks different depending on what you sell — but these three cover the most common solopreneur business models.

**Notion AI** for:
- Service businesses: SOPs, client documentation, proposal templates, project management
- Coaching/consulting: Meeting notes → action items → client deliverables
- Digital products: Outlining and drafting course content, eBooks

**Descript** for:
- Video course creators: Edit videos by editing the transcript
- Podcasters: Remove filler words, add captions, create social clips
- Coaches: Record client session → generate summary → clip key moments

**Loom** for:
- Async client communication (faster than email, more personal than text)
- Onboarding videos instead of live calls
- Tutorial content that's easier to record than write

### 4. Operations: Claude + Notion + Stripe

**Monthly cost**: ~$35
**Time saved**: 3–4 hours/week

Operations is where most solopreneurs leak the most time — invoicing, contracts, admin, planning.

**Claude for operations:**
- Draft contracts and client agreements (then have a lawyer review)
- Analyze your financials and explain what they mean
- Write job descriptions if you're starting to hire contractors
- Create SOPs for any repeatable process

Use this prompt monthly:
> "Here's my revenue and expense data for [month]: [paste]. Analyze it and tell me: What were my top 3 revenue sources? Where am I overspending? What patterns do you see? What should I focus on next month?"

**Notion as your operating system:**
- Weekly planning and review
- Client tracker
- Project management
- Content calendar
- Financial dashboard

**Stripe for payments:**
- One-time products
- Subscriptions
- Payment links (no website needed for quick sales)
- Automatic invoicing

---

### 5. Customer Support: Tidio + Make + Claude

**Monthly cost**: ~$50–80
**Time saved**: 5–10 hours/week

When you're solo, customer support can eat your entire day if you let it. This stack prevents that.

**Tidio** (with Lyro AI) handles the 70% of support questions that are repetitive:
- Order status
- Refund policy
- How to access their purchase
- FAQ-style questions

**Make** handles routing and follow-up:
- New support ticket → categorize → assign priority → notify you in Slack
- Resolved ticket → trigger NPS survey after 3 days
- Refund request → create task → send acknowledgment email

**Claude** for the 30% that needs a human:
> "Here's a customer complaint: [paste email]. Write a response that's empathetic, takes responsibility where appropriate, offers a clear solution, and maintains our brand voice (direct, genuine, no corporate speak)."

You still write the response — but Claude does the drafting work.

## What the Stack Doesn't Replace

Be clear-eyed about this: AI doesn't replace:

- **Your unique expertise and perspective** — This is your moat
- **Relationship building** — Nobody is outsourcing trust
- **Strategic judgment** — AI advises; you decide
- **Accountability** — The business is still yours

The solopreneurs who thrive with this stack aren't the ones trying to hide that they're a team of one. They're the ones who own it — and use AI to deliver the quality of a team without the overhead.

## Getting Started

If you're new to AI tools and this stack feels overwhelming, here's the order to implement it in:

**Week 1–2**: Claude Pro only. Use it for everything — writing, planning, thinking.
**Week 3–4**: Add Buffer. Start scheduling content consistently.
**Month 2**: Add Make. Automate one repetitive workflow.
**Month 3**: Add Tidio. Set up AI support for the top 10 questions you get.
**Month 4+**: Add Surfer and Descript as your content volume grows.

You don't need the whole stack on day one. You need to build the habits that the stack supports.
