Every week, small business owners spend hours on tasks that could run themselves. Copy-pasting data between apps. Manually sending the same email. Downloading a spreadsheet just to upload it somewhere else.

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is the tool that fixes this. And unlike Zapier, it's powerful enough to handle complex, multi-step automations — while still being visual enough for non-developers to build.

This guide gets you up and running with your first automation in under 2 hours.

## What Is Make.com?

Make is a **visual automation platform** that connects 1,500+ apps through a drag-and-drop workflow builder.

Think of it like building a flowchart that runs on autopilot. You define:
- **Trigger**: What starts the automation (a new email, a form submission, a scheduled time)
- **Actions**: What happens in response (create a record, send a message, update a spreadsheet)
- **Filters**: Conditions that control which data gets processed

The visual canvas makes it easy to see your entire workflow at once — something Zapier's linear interface can't match.

> A **Scenario** in Make = an automation workflow

## Core Concepts Before You Build

### Modules
Every step in a Make scenario is a **module** — a block that either watches for data, transforms data, or sends data somewhere.

Types of modules:
- **Trigger modules** (watch): Start the scenario. Example: "Watch for new emails in Gmail"
- **Action modules**: Do something. Example: "Create a row in Google Sheets"
- **Search modules**: Look something up. Example: "Search for a contact in HubSpot"
- **Aggregator modules**: Combine multiple items into one
- **Iterator modules**: Break one item into multiple

### Operations
Make's free plan includes **1,000 operations/month**. Each module run = 1 operation. A scenario with 4 modules running 50 times = 200 operations.

### Scheduling
Scenarios can run:
- **Immediately** when triggered (webhooks)
- On a schedule (every 15 minutes, hourly, daily)

## Your First Automation: Contact Form → CRM + Slack

Let's build a real, useful automation: when a lead fills out your contact form, automatically add them to your CRM and notify your team in Slack.

**What you need:**
- A Typeform or Tally.so form
- A CRM (HubSpot free tier works)
- A Slack workspace

### Step 1: Create a New Scenario

1. Click **"Create a new scenario"**
2. You'll see a blank canvas with a central "+" button
3. Click the "+" to add your first module

### Step 2: Set the Trigger

1. Search for **"Typeform"** and select it
2. Choose **"Watch Responses"**
3. Click **"Add"** to connect your Typeform account (OAuth — takes 30 seconds)
4. Select your contact form from the dropdown
5. Set the trigger to run on a schedule (every 15 minutes is fine to start)

### Step 3: Add a Filter (Optional but Recommended)

Add a filter between your trigger and actions to only process legitimate submissions:

1. Click the small wrench icon between modules
2. Add condition: `Email` → `Contains` → `@`

This prevents test submissions from cluttering your CRM.

### Step 4: Add Your CRM Action

1. Click the "+" after your trigger to add a new module
2. Search for **"HubSpot CRM"** → **"Create a Contact"**
3. Map your form fields to HubSpot contact properties:
   - Name field → `firstname` + `lastname`
   - Email field → `email`
   - Message field → `message` (custom property)
   - Form source → `lead_source` = "Website Contact Form"

### Step 5: Add Slack Notification

1. Add another module: **"Slack"** → **"Create a Message"**
2. Connect your Slack account
3. Select your `#leads` channel (or create one)
4. Write your message with dynamic variables:

```
🔔 New Lead: {{1.answers.name.text}}
📧 {{1.answers.email.email}}
📝 {{1.answers.message.text}}
⏰ {{now}}
```

### Step 6: Add Error Handling

Real automation needs error handling. If HubSpot is down and the contact doesn't get created, you want to know.

1. Right-click the HubSpot module → **"Add error handler"**
2. Choose **"Resume"** → Add a Slack message to `#errors`:
   > "⚠️ Failed to create HubSpot contact for {{email}}"

### Step 7: Test Your Scenario

1. Click **"Run once"** in the bottom toolbar
2. Submit a test entry to your form
3. Watch the data flow through your scenario in real-time
4. Check HubSpot — your test contact should appear
5. Check Slack — your notification should be there

### Step 8: Activate Your Scenario

Once the test passes:
1. Click the **"OFF"** toggle to turn it **"ON"**
2. Set your schedule (every 15 minutes recommended)
3. Click **"Save"**

Your automation is live. 🎉

## Troubleshooting Common Make Issues

**Problem**: Scenario runs but no data processes
**Fix**: Check your trigger schedule. Make's free tier runs on a 15-minute minimum interval. Switch to webhooks for instant triggers.

**Problem**: Module shows error on connection
**Fix**: Reconnect the app (OAuth tokens expire). In the module, click the connection dropdown → "Add" → reconnect.

**Problem**: Data mapping isn't working
**Fix**: Run the scenario once manually to populate the variable picker, then re-map your fields.

**Problem**: Scenario runs too often / operations running out
**Fix**: Add filters early in the scenario to stop irrelevant data from flowing through.
