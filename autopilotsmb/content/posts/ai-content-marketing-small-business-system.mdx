Content marketing is the long game. The businesses that win on Google, LinkedIn, and social media in 2025 are producing content at a volume that used to require an entire marketing team.

AI doesn't replace your content strategy — but it does make one person capable of executing like a team.

Here's the exact system I recommend to small business owners who want to build a content engine without burning out.

## The Content Pyramid

Before any AI tool enters the picture, understand this framework: **the content pyramid**.

```
         [Long-form Content]
             Blog posts
           Pillar pages
          Video scripts
         ─────────────────
        [Medium-form Content]
          Email newsletters
          LinkedIn articles
          Twitter threads
       ─────────────────────
      [Short-form Content]
        Social posts
        Quote cards
        Stories/Reels
```

Every long-form piece you create should generate 5–10 pieces of medium and short-form content. AI makes this repurposing instant.

## The Weekly Content Engine

This is the weekly system that produces ~30 pieces of content from a single source:

### Monday: Core Content Day
**Time**: 2–3 hours (you) + AI assistance

1. **Choose a topic** from your editorial calendar (based on keyword research)
2. **Create a voice-note outline** — talk through your main points for 5–10 minutes (use Otter.ai to transcribe)
3. **Paste transcript into Claude** with this prompt:
   > "Based on these rough notes, create a detailed outline for a 2,000-word blog post targeting [keyword]. Include H2s, key points per section, and specific examples to include."
4. **Write the actual post** — use the outline as a scaffold, write in your own voice, use AI to expand weak sections
5. **Run through Surfer SEO** to check on-page optimization

**Output**: 1 complete blog post (2,000–3,000 words)

### Tuesday: Repurpose Day
**Time**: 1–2 hours

Use this Claude prompt:
> "Take this blog post and create: (1) a 5-email nurture sequence about this topic for subscribers, (2) a 10-tweet thread summarizing the key insights, (3) a LinkedIn article version (800 words), (4) 5 short social media captions (150 words each) for Instagram and Facebook."

**Output**: 1 email sequence (5 emails) + 1 Twitter thread + 1 LinkedIn article + 5 social captions

### Wednesday: Visual Day
**Time**: 30–60 minutes

Use Canva AI to create:
- Cover image for the blog post
- Quote cards (3–5) from the best lines in the article
- Pinterest pins (2–3)
- Instagram carousel template

**Output**: 10–15 visual assets

### Thursday: Distribution Day
**Time**: 30–45 minutes

- Publish and schedule social posts via Buffer or Publer
- Send newsletter (repurposed email from Tuesday)
- Submit blog post to relevant content roundups
- Repurpose Twitter thread to LinkedIn

### Friday: Analytics & Planning Day
**Time**: 30–45 minutes

- Check what performed well this week
- Update your editorial calendar for next month
- Research 3 new keyword opportunities using Ahrefs or Semrush

**Total weekly time investment**: 5–7 hours for ~30 pieces of content

## The Brand Voice Problem (And How to Solve It)

The number one complaint I hear about AI content: "It doesn't sound like me."

Here's the fix: **build a brand voice document** before you prompt anything.

Create a file called `brand-voice.md` with:

```markdown
## Tone
- Confident but not arrogant
- Direct, no filler phrases
- Uses data and examples, not vague claims
- Occasional dry humor, never sarcasm

## Things we say
- "The truth is..."
- "Here's what actually works..."
- "Let's be specific..."

## Things we never say
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Game-changer"
- "Paradigm shift"
- Excessive exclamation points!!!

## Audience
- Solopreneurs and small business owners (1–10 employees)
- Time-poor, results-focused
- Smart but not necessarily technical
- Skeptical of hype, open to proof

## Examples of content we love
[Paste 3–5 examples of your existing writing]
```

Include this document at the start of every AI prompt, or save it as a custom instruction in Claude.

## SEO + AI: The Winning Combination

AI writing without SEO intent produces content that nobody finds. Here's how to combine them:

### Step 1: Keyword Research First
Use Ahrefs, Semrush, or the free **Google Search Console** to find:
- Keywords you already rank for (positions 4–20 — easy wins to optimize)
- Keywords your competitors rank for that you don't
- Long-tail variations with clear commercial intent

**Target**: 1 primary keyword per post, 3–5 secondary keywords

### Step 2: Analyze the SERP
Before writing, search your target keyword. Analyze the top 5 results:
- What's their word count?
- What H2 sections do they all include?
- What questions do they NOT answer? (Your opportunity)

Use Claude to speed this up:
> "I want to rank for [keyword]. Here are the titles and meta descriptions of the top 10 results: [paste them]. What gaps do you see in this content? What angle would help a new article stand out?"

### Step 3: Write with Surfer
Surfer SEO shows you exactly which terms, headers, and structure to include to match what Google expects. Use it while writing — treat it as a checklist, not a rulebook.

### Step 4: Internal Linking at Scale
Once you have 20+ posts, internal linking becomes powerful but time-consuming. Use this Claude prompt monthly:
> "Here's a list of my published blog posts: [titles and slugs]. Here's my new post: [content]. Suggest 5–8 internal linking opportunities with the exact anchor text to use."

## The Mindset Shift

The businesses that struggle with AI content marketing make one mistake: they try to use AI to avoid the work of thinking.

The businesses that win use AI to **execute faster on better ideas**.

You still need to:
- Understand your audience deeply
- Have a genuine point of view
- Know your topic well enough to fact-check the output
- Edit ruthlessly

AI is the accelerant. You're still the engine.

Start with one blog post per week using this system. Master the workflow. Then scale.
